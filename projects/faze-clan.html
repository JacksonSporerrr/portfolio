FaZe Clan Insights Intern Case Study

Creator scouting strategy, talent agency toolset analysis, and branded campaign reporting for FaZe Clan — one of the world's largest esports and content creator organizations.

Python Excel Tableau CreatorIQ Data Visualization

Overview

This case study simulates the role of a data insights intern at FaZe Clan's talent agency. The project had three components: (1) evaluating the toolset a modern talent agency uses to manage creators and campaigns, (2) identifying the next breakout streamers through a data-driven scouting analysis, and (3) producing a performance report on a real branded campaign — FaZe Clan x CashApp.

Tools & Technologies

Excel Tableau Python CreatorIQ Hootsuite Looker Studio Meta Ads Manager

Dataset

Data was sourced from public streaming analytics (Twitch viewer counts, peak concurrent viewers during subathon events), TikTok view counts for top creators, and FaZe Clan's historical impression and engagement metrics. Viewer distribution and gender demographic data was pulled from publicly available creator analytics dashboards.

Process

Part 1 — Talent Agency Toolset

Mapped the full workflow of a modern creator talent agency, identifying best-in-class tools for each stage: CreatorIQ for talent management and campaign matching, Hootsuite and native platform tools for media distribution, Excel/Tableau/Looker Studio for data reporting, Juro for contract automation, and Notion for content creation workflows.

Part 2 — Creator Scouting

Analyzed streaming and social media data to identify three undervalued creators as candidates for FaZe Clan recruitment or partnership:

  • Pilat — averaged 729 viewers on Fortnite content but demonstrated outsized appeal in collaborative streams. Appeared in Lacy's subathon events where he consistently drove the highest peak viewer counts of any featured creator, reaching 40,000–50,000 concurrent viewers.
  • Marlon — averages ~3,500 viewers, comparable to several FaZe members pre-signing. Born in Sweden with a diverse audience spanning Scandinavia and North America (21% Swedish, 36% US). Strong TikTok-adjacent appeal and 33% female audience — a demographic FaZe has historically underindexed.
  • Leah Halton — one of the most-viewed TikTokers globally (1.2B views on most viral content). Identified as a candidate for FaZe's umbrella agency to expand female representation and reach a non-gaming Gen-Z audience. 95%+ female viewer base offers brand partnership diversification.

Part 3 — Campaign Report: FaZe x CashApp

Produced a full performance report on FaZe Clan's CashApp branded campaign, analyzing platform breakdown, impressions, engagement rate, and app installs against campaign targets. Identified which content formats drove results and provided actionable recommendations.

Impressions driven by TikTok

The CashApp campaign exceeded every target metric. YouTube integrated content and live stream segments were the strongest drivers of brand awareness and engagement. TikTok drove the largest share of total impressions, while ad integration within videos — rather than standalone ad reads — drove the majority of app installs.

For creator scouting, data analysis revealed that Pilat was significantly undervalued based on average viewer count alone — his collaborative performance metrics told a very different story. Marlon's pre-FaZe viewership was on par with existing FaZe members at their time of signing.

Business Insights

Creator scouting based purely on average viewership misses high-upside talent. Collaborative performance metrics — how a creator performs when featured alongside established names — are a better signal for breakout potential. For branded campaigns, authentic integration outperforms hard ad reads: audiences respond to creators who use the product naturally in content, driving both higher engagement rates and more app installs than traditional sponsorship formats. Short-form experimentation and challenge-style content are the clearest levers for future growth.
