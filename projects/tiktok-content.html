Overview

Outside of academics, I built a TikTok presence from scratch around competitive Fortnite content. Using Adobe Premiere Pro for editing, I produced and posted short-form videos — developing my own style, understanding what content resonated with an audience, and iterating based on performance. The result: millions of views and a following of over 10,000 people, built without a team or ad spend.

Tools & Skills

Adobe Premiere Pro TikTok Content Strategy Video Editing Audience Analytics Personal Branding

Process

  • Self-taught Adobe Premiere Pro to produce professional-quality short-form edits of gameplay footage.
  • Developed a consistent posting cadence and content style that built audience recognition over time.
  • Analyzed TikTok performance metrics (watch time, completion rate, share rate) to understand what drove viral reach and applied those learnings to future content.
  • Iterated on hook timing, pacing, audio selection, and caption strategy based on engagement data.
  • Managed the full production pipeline independently — capture, edit, export, post, and analyze — treating the channel as a small media business.

Business Insights

Running a content channel is running a small business. Every post is a hypothesis — you're testing a hook, a format, a topic — and the metrics tell you whether it worked. The creators and brands who win on short-form platforms treat it as a data problem: they post consistently, measure rigorously, and iterate fast. This experience gave me firsthand fluency in the creator economy that directly informed the FaZe Clan case study — I understand both the data side and the creative side of what makes content perform.
